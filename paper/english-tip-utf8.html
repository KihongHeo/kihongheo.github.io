Tips on English

a/an/the 사용법

  • "language ML", "rule A" ==> 이런 순서면 the는 붙이지않는다.
  • "the ML language", "the A rule" ==> 이런 순서일 때 the를 붙인다.
  • "single X" 앞에는 항상 "a":
    • "Every function has *a* single argument." (o)
  • a/an/the의 차이
    • 관사가 없으면: 지명적인 정도 0
    • a/an : 지명적인 정도 1 "저기 있잖아...", "저기...", "그런..."
    • the : 지명적인 정도 2 "그...", "바로 그..."
      "Consider *the* C program in Figure 1."
  • 약어 앞에 "the"를 붙이나?
    • 약어가 고유명사를 지칭하면 앞에 "the" 붙이지 않는다.
    • 약어가 일반명사를 지칭하면 앞에 "the"를 붙인다.
    • 약어를 도입하고 뒤에 사용할 때는 약어앞에 "the"를 붙인다.
      "Projection expression (PE) is ..., ... the PE is ..."

this/it/that 사용법

  • "this", "that"은 단독으로 그것만 사용하지 말고 실제 대상을 꼭 따라붙인다:
    • "This is ..." (x)
    • "This result is ..." (o)
    • "That is strange." (x)
    • "That result is strange." (o)
  • "it" 을 대상을 지칭하는 것으로 쓰지 말것.
    • "I don't use the environment; it is completely abstracted."(x)
      애매하다. "it"이 무엇을 지칭하는가? "environment"? 아니면 "I don't use..."라는 사실?
  • 비슷하게, "the above" 사용을 자제하라. "above" 는 너무 애매하다.

명심할 용법

  • "require"는 목적어 2개: 무엇 & 누구를.
    • "It requires to finish the homework." (x)
    • 이렇게 쓰면 "숙제를 끝내는 것은 []을 요구한다".
    • "It requires us to finish the homework." (o)
  • "render"는 "make"와는 달리, 항상 "render + 목적어 + 형용사".
    • "Our new idea renders it smooth." (o)
    • "Our new idea renders a clear result." (x)
  • "due to"는 항상 be 동사와 같이.
    • "It is due to A." (o)
    • "Limitation due to A is ..." (x)
  • "consider" vs "regard"
    • "I consider tea (no "as") my favorite."
    • "He was considered (no "as") a genius."
    • "I regard him as a genius."
  • "appreciate" 용법
    • "I would appreciate *it*(always) if you ...". (o)
    • "I would appreciate your doing ...". (o)
    • "It would be appreciated if ...". (x)
    • "Your prompt payment would be appreciated." (o)
  • "such as" 와 "like"
    • "such as"는 "for example"로 바꾸어서 말이되면 된다.
    • "like"는 "similar to" 로 바꾸어서 말이되면 된다.
    • "AT&T locations, like MH, already use ..." 에서 "like" 는 "such as" 여야 한다.
  • "It requires to *measure carefully* the cost again." (순서? O.K.)
    "... must *be repeatedly* designed and tested." (순서? O.K.)
  • "... languages like ML, SCHEME and *even* C" (C는 아니라고 생각할 수 있지만 "C 마저도...")
  • "various" vs "varied"
    • "various"는 항상 복수 명사가 따라온다.
    • "a various balance" (x), "various balances" (o)
    • "varied"는 "is"다음에 오는 형용사.
    • "Their colors are varied." (o) "Their colors are various." (x)
    • "They have various colors." (o) "They have varied colors." (x)
  • "either/or"의 위치
    • "It should either be A or B." (o)
    • "It should be either A or B." (o)
    • "It should be involved either in A or in B." (o)
    • "It should be involved in either A or B." (o)
  • "should/must"의 차이
    • must, have to: (requiring) 강하다
    • should, ought to: (optional) must보다 약하다.
  • "usage" 사용자제, 대신 "use"를 사용한다
    • "It is tested for safety without the use of animals."
  • "efficacy" vs "efficiency"
    • efficacy, effectiveness: 효험, 효력, 유효, 효과
    • efficiency: 능률, 능력, 유능, 유효, 효율
  • "except for" vs "except that"
    • except for : except for that, except for this idea
    • except that : except that he is young
  • "the previous one" vs "the former"
    • This approach is better than the former.: 그 내용이 좀전에 언급되었으면 "the former"를 사용할 것
    • This approach is better than the previous one.: 그렇지 않은 경우는 "the previous one", eg) "예전 works들은 ...", "예전 paper들이 ..."
  • need와 only
    • "We *need only show that* either A is true or B is false."
  • "present"(show) 다음에 곧바로 "how to solve/when to do/..."등이 따라오지 않고, 명사가 따라온다.
    • He presented how to stop the crime.: (x)
    • He presented his idea how to stop the crime.: (o)
  • "ask"의 사용
    • "I ask a question to you." (x)
    • "I ask you a question." (o)
    • "ask"동사는 다음의 용법을 따르지 않는다:
    • "I send a letter to you." (o)
    • "I send you a letter." (o)
  • "following" 용법
    • "The following information is..."
    • "... as follows:"
    • "... in the following."
  • "non" vs "un"
    • 보통 in-, un-은 `반대'의 뜻을 나타내고, non-은 `부정, 결여'의 뜻을 나타냄.
    • non-military personal: 일반인
    • unmilitary personal: 오합지졸 당나라병사
    • non-conventional analyses
    • un-conventional analyses
  • "guarantee"의 용법
    • "Generated analyzer *is guaranteed to*(수동) have the polynomial complexity.": (o)
    • "I *guarantees*(능동) to have ...": (x)
  • "guarantee" 와 "need" 는 개인적인 느낌이다. 다른 단어를 사용하도록.
  • "assure"는 "assure that ..."으로.
    • "They can *assure* their programs to have ..." (x)
    • "They can assure that their programs have ..." (o)
  • "much" 는 긍정문에서는 사용하지 않는다.
    • "He makes much money." (x)
    • "He makes a lot of money." (o)
    • "Does he make much money?" (o)
    • "He doesn't make much money." (o)
  • "evaluate"는 능동으로 쓴다.
    • "Expression e *evaluates* to k." (o)
    • "Expression e *is evaluated* to k." (x)
  • "right" vs "immediately"
    • "The value *right* before the beginning ..." (x)
    • "The value *immediately* before the beginning ..." (o)
  • "enough"는 항상 "형용사 enough to"로 쓴다.
    • "It's enough to prove ..." (x)
    • "It's sufficient to prove ..." (o)
    • "Two things are *enough* to guarantee ..." (x)
    • "Two things are *sufficient* to guarantee ..." (o)
  • "consists of"
    • "A consists of two things." (x)
    • "A consists of two parts." (o)
  • "tired" vs "tiring"
    • "I'm tired of getting this mail." 지겹다.
    • "I'm tiring of getting this mail." 지겨워지고 있다. 좀 더 참을 수는 있다.
  • *a number of*는 "many"보다는 neutral한 의미의(unbiased) "some"을 의미
  • "as"는 because 대신으로 "즉", "왜냐하면"의 뉘앙스. because 보다는 *약한* 느낌으로. "... 그게 이래서 그래 ..."
    • "A and B are finite, *as* |A| = n < infinite and |B| = |A| + 1."
  • "As a similar example, consider ..." (o)
  • "For a similar example, consider ..." (o)
  • 논문에 "need" 사용 자제.
    • "need"의 뉴앙스는 말하는 사람의 감정이 관련되는 느낌이된다.
    • "need to be " 는 사용 자제.
  • In conclusion section
    • "I have discussed ..."라고 하면 현장감 있다.
    • "I discussed ..."라고 하면 시간적으로 많이 동 떨어진 느낌이다.
  • It is much easier to *do using method A*.: (o)
  • It is much easier to do *by* using method A.: (x)
  • "change"는 바꾼다(switch)의 의미.
    변화시키다는 "modify/alter".
  • "involve"는 좀 느슨한 동사다. 사용자제.
  • "As you knew, ..."는 이상하다. 항상 현재형으로: "As you know, ..." (o)
  • ", which ..."
    • 필요없어도 되는 부연설명일 때
    • 필요한, 제한하는 내용이 따라붙어야 할 때는 "that..."을 쓴다
  • 수식어와 수식되는 것을 항상 가까이.
    • "It is an almost impossible task." (o)
    • "It is almost an impossible task." (x)
  • "as best they can" 사용하면
    • they가 원래부터 좀 불구이거나 모자라는 느낌의 뉴앙스가 된다.
  • since 다음에 반드시 과거형이 오는 것은 아니다. 동사의 성격에 따라 현재 완료를 써야 한다.
    "Three problems have occurred since..."
    • "... I enrolled VISA card program." (o)
    • "... I have had VISA card program." (o)
    • "... I had VISA card program." (x)
  • "Ours are different in that ... and in that ..."
  • "... in as much detail as they can"
  • "We call finitary an inference system whenever the associated system is finitary."
  • "In C, not all accessible memory locations are named by program variables."
  • "For brevity, ... are omitted from ... in this chapter."
  • "A is planned for April 5th 1994."
  • "We can decide whether the expression is involved in the exception behavior *or not*."
    ==> "We can decide whether or not the expression is involved in the exception behavior."
  • "We repeat the process in a fixed number of times."
    ==> "We repeate the process a fixed number of times."
  • "In Section 3 we conclude with *discussing* future works."
    ==> "In Section 3 we conclude with future works."
  • "This method *helps them*, by forcing ...[long], *to keep* ..."
    ==> "This method, by forcing ..., *helps them to keep* ..."
    "Better to put tight clauses together."
  • "This two are necessary *for the analysis termination*."
    ==> "This two are necessary *for the analysis to terminate*."
  • "Every chain between x and y are of the same length."
    ==> "Every pair of chains between x and y have the same length."
  • "We won't present *the real* algorithm *in details*."
    ==> "We won't present *the full details of* the algorithm."
  • "We will discuss Z1's future extensions."
    ==> "We will discuss some possible extensions *to* Z1."
  • "teach it to wider audience in earlier curriculum"
    ==> "teach it to a wider audience earlier in the curriculum"
  • "Size |s| of string s" ==> "The size |s| of string s"
  • "Natural way to approach it" ==> "The natural way to approach it"
  • "As illustrated in [10] we may leave" ==> "As illustrated in [10], we may leave"
  • "Base cases" => "The base cases"
  • "Inductive cases" => "The inductive cases"
  • "easy to see because ..." => "easy to see, because ..."
  • "connection to the practice" => "connection to practice"
  • error/bug report의 좋은 영어 예
    • "I imagine I am not the first to alert you to this - can you give me a pointer to corrected sources?"
